The start of the war

Over the years, historians have argued about when the war started.Although the war officaly started in 1939, some say that World war II started as early as 1933 when Hitler took power, or as late as 1941, when the USA entered the war against Japan. Here is a list of dates that historians say that the war started on:

  • 1933, When Adolf Hitler took power in Germany and became a dictator
  • 1937, When The Imperial Japanese army invaded Manchuria.
  • 1939 The war offically started in this year, when Hitler invaded poland.
  • 1940, When the Soviet Union joined the war*.
  • 1941, When the USA joined the war

The beginning of the European Theater

The the war in Europe started When Germany invaded Poland on September 1st, 1939. After the invasion, Great britain and France, allies of Poland Declared war on Germany. Germany invaded Poland because of three reasons:

  1. Germany was humiliated by their defeat in World War I.
  2. The wanted more 'Lebensraum'(or living space) so that the could resettle farmers in occupied territorry and boost the economy.

The Bombing of Britain

After the fall of France Hitler wanted to attack Great Britain. But, instead of just invaded the island, the Nazis chose to attack it with bombing raids. In the autum of 1940 the Luftwaffe (german air force) started to bomb British ports and shipping harbours. Hitler proposed an ultimatum, but britain refused. The Luftwaffe was unable to defeat the RAF Fighter Command (part of the royal air force), so the germans used the strategic bombing tactic. The Nazis started to bomb London and other cities during the Blitz, but the germans were not able to destroy british morale. The bombing ended in the spring of 1941.

Attack on the Soviet Union (1941)

After Hitler had taken over Poland, Belgium, Luxembourg, Denmark, The Netherlands, and France, he was sure the only reason that Britain was not surrendering was that it hoped for aid from the Soviet Union. So, the Nazis were going to try and knock out the Soviet Union in two ways:

  1. Make them join the Axis
  2. Take over the Soviet Union

The Nazis tried option 1 first.

Negotiations took place between germany and soviet union. The Soviet union accepted but in return, the soviets wanted concessions from Finland, Bulgaria, Turkey, and Japan to which Germany refused (beacause Bulgaria and Japan were part of the Axis). On 18 December 1940, Hitler ordered to plan for an invasion of the soviet union.On 22 June 1941, during Operation Barbarossa, The Nazis invaded the Soviet Union. The objectives of the invasion were to eliminate the Soviet Union as a military power, exterminate Communism, generate Lebensraum by dispossessing the native population and guarantee access to the strategic resources needed to defeat Germany's remaining rivals. Many key battles happened during the german advance into soviet territorry, such as Leningrad, Stalingrad, and many others.
